Comal County Commissioners Address Growing Road Repair Needs in Budget Review

February 6, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

Comal County Commissioners Court reviewed a substantial allocation for county road repairs during a recent budget discussion, focusing on the infrastructure needs created by rapid population growth in the county's fastest-developing areas.

The proposed million allocation for road maintenance and improvements prioritizes routes in precincts 1 and 3, which have experienced the heaviest traffic increases due to new residential and commercial development. Commissioners emphasized that the county's road system has faced unprecedented wear as growth continues to outpace infrastructure capacity.

"Our roads are taking a beating from increased traffic volume and heavier construction vehicles," said one commissioner during the budget workshop. "We need to get ahead of the maintenance curve before minor problems become major reconstruction projects."

The road repair allocation represents part of Comal County's broader infrastructure investment strategy as officials grapple with managing a million budget approved last year, up from million in 2025. The county's tax rate increased to 30.5 cents per valuation to accommodate growing service demands driven by population growth.

Precinct-Specific Priorities

Under the county's unit road system, each precinct commissioner manages road maintenance for their area. The proposed funding would allow precincts 1 and 3 to address priority routes that serve as main thoroughfares for new developments while distributing remaining funds across all four precincts for ongoing maintenance needs.

The commissioners are expected to finalize the road repair budget allocation as part of their broader county infrastructure planning process, with implementation beginning in the spring construction season.

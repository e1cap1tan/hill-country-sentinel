Comal County Commissioner Race Heats Up with Three-Way Republican Primary

February 14, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff
<p>In the upcoming Republican primary for Comal County Commissioner Precinct 2, three candidates are vying for the position, setting the stage for a competitive race that could shape local infrastructure and development policies. Incumbent Sarah Martinez is seeking a second term, facing challenges from local business owner Tom Rodriguez and former school board member Jennifer Walsh. With early voting beginning February 14 and the primary election on March 5, 2026, voters in Precinct 2 have a crucial opportunity to influence the county's future direction.</p><p>Sarah Martinez, who has served as commissioner since 2023, has focused her tenure on infrastructure improvements, including road maintenance and water resource management. Supporters praise her hands-on approach to addressing the county's rapid growth, but critics argue that more aggressive action is needed to keep pace with population increases. Martinez's campaign emphasizes continuity and experience, highlighting projects like the expansion of FM 306 and enhancements to county water systems.</p><p>Challenging Martinez is Tom Rodriguez, a construction industry veteran who brings private sector experience to the race. Rodriguez argues that his business background equips him to streamline county operations and attract economic development. "Comal County needs efficient governance to support our growing families and businesses," Rodriguez stated in a recent interview. His platform includes proposals for faster permitting processes and partnerships with private entities for infrastructure projects.</p><p>Jennifer Walsh, drawing from her time on the local school board, positions herself as a candidate focused on education and community services. Walsh's experience advocating for school funding and youth programs informs her approach to county-wide issues like development growth and public safety. "As commissioner, I'll ensure that our growth benefits all residents, especially our children and families," she said. Walsh has garnered support from education advocates who see her as a fresh perspective on balancing expansion with quality of life.</p><p>Key issues in the race include road maintenance, water infrastructure, and managing development growth amid Comal County's booming population. All three candidates agree on the need for improved roadways and sustainable water policies, but differ on the pace and methods of implementation. The winner of the Republican primary will face any Democratic challengers in the general election, though Precinct 2 has historically leaned conservative.</p><p>With the filing deadline approaching, the race is expected to intensify as candidates ramp up outreach efforts. Voters in Precinct 2, which encompasses parts of New Braunfels and surrounding areas, will play a decisive role in determining who advances to represent their interests on the Commissioners Court. The Hill Country Sentinel will provide ongoing coverage of the campaign, including candidate forums and policy positions.</p>

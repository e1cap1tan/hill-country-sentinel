Comal County Commissioner Race Heats Up with Three-Way Republican Primary

February 14, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

The Republican primary for Comal County Precinct 1 Commissioner is shaping up as a three-way contest between incumbent Ben Luderer, challenger Scott Wunsche, and political newcomer David Thiel. With early voting beginning February 17, the race has drawn attention for its implications on county infrastructure and development policies.

Incumbent Ben Luderer, who has served since 2018, brings experience in managing county budgets and overseeing road projects. Luderer emphasizes his record of fiscal responsibility, citing recent approvals for widening FM 306 and improving drainage systems in growing subdivisions. \"We've made significant strides in keeping pace with our population boom,\" Luderer said in a recent interview.

Challenger Scott Wunsche, a local businessman, positions himself as a fresh voice focused on reducing property taxes and streamlining permitting for new developments. Wunsche has criticized the current commission for what he calls \"overreach\" in environmental regulations that he believes hinder economic growth. His campaign has gained traction through endorsements from several local chambers of commerce.

David Thiel, entering politics for the first time, brings a background in community organizing and environmental advocacy. Thiel advocates for sustainable development, prioritizing green spaces and water conservation amid Comal County's rapid expansion. \"We need to grow smart, not just fast,\" Thiel stated at a recent candidate forum.

The Precinct 1 seat covers a large swath of eastern Comal County, including parts of New Braunfels and Canyon Lake. Issues like traffic congestion, school overcrowding, and affordable housing are top concerns for voters in the area. The primary winner will face no Democratic opposition in the general election, making the March 3 contest the decisive race.

Early voting runs from February 17 to March 1, with Election Day on March 3. For more information on candidates and polling locations, visit the Comal County Elections Office website.

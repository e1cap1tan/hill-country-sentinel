Local Restaurant Group Announces Support for Conservative Municipal Candidates

February 7, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

A prominent New Braunfels restaurant group publicly announced donations to multiple conservative candidates running in the upcoming municipal election, drawing both praise and criticism across social media platforms as the business community increasingly engages in local politics.

The restaurant chain, which operates several locations throughout New Braunfels and surrounding areas, disclosed contributions to candidates supporting traditional values and limited government policies. The donations comply with Texas campaign finance laws, which place no statewide contribution limits on municipal elections, allowing businesses to support candidates of their choice.

"We believe in supporting candidates who share our values and understand the challenges facing small businesses," a company spokesperson said in response to community questions about the donations. "Our involvement in local elections reflects our commitment to the community we serve."

Mixed Community Response

The announcement sparked discussion on local social media groups, with some residents applauding the business for taking a public stance while others questioned whether restaurants should engage in political activities. The debate reflects broader conversations happening across Texas communities about the role of businesses in local politics.

Supporters praised the restaurant group for transparency in their political involvement and for backing candidates who they believe will support business-friendly policies. Critics expressed concern about businesses using their platform to influence elections, arguing that restaurants should remain politically neutral to serve all customers equally.

The donations are documented in campaign finance reports filed with local election officials, as required by Texas law. Similar business contributions to municipal candidates have become increasingly common across the state as local elections take on greater significance in shaping community development and economic policy.

Residents Demand State Block Canyon Lake-Area Wastewater Plant

February 11, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

Comal County residents are pressing the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ) to block a proposed wastewater treatment plant near Canyon Lake, citing environmental and development concerns.

The facility is part of permits for new development projects in the area, but residents argue it should not be evaluated in isolation.

During public comment periods, residents called for the agency to assess the plant cumulatively alongside permits for other developments and discharges in the region, rather than reviewing it as a standalone project.

Several speakers also called for a moratorium on new development in the area and throughout Comal County, reflecting growing concerns about rapid growth's impact on infrastructure and the environment.

As part of the TCEQ process, the agency will provide a written response to all public comments before making a decision on whether the application meets state legal requirements. That ruling will then be considered by the three TCEQ commissioners for final approval.

The controversy highlights ongoing tensions between development pressures and environmental protection in one of Texas's fastest-growing counties.

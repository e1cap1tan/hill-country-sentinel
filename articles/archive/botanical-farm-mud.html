# New Braunfels City Council approves 250-acre Botanical Farm Municipal Utility District **Date:** Feb 26, 2026 **Source:** New Braunfels Herald-Zeitung On Feb. 9, the New Braunfels City Council passed a resolution consenting to the creation of the Botanical Farm Municipal Utility District (MUD), which encompasses two properties totaling 252.16 acres. The MUD sits in the city's extra territorial jurisdiction (ETJ) in Guadalupe County and will be located near the Altwein and Elmons Road intersection, according to the resolution. Additionally, the city entered a development agreement with O Union Wine Rd, LLC, for single-family residential use within the MUD. The agreement states that the development must align with the city’s comprehensive plan, Envision New Braunfels, which encourages balancing fiscally responsible growth and preventing undue fiscal burdens on the city, the agreement states. A MUD is an independent political subdivision of Texas that finances infrastructure through bonds repaid by taxes and fees collected from property owners within the district. Initially controlled by landowners or developers, the district will later be governed by a board elected by district residents and is regulated by the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ).

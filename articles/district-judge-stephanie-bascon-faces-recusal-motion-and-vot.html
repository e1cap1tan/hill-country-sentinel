District Judge Stephanie Bascon Faces Recusal Motion and Voter Warning Campaign

February 22, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

COMAL COUNTY, TX — A recusal motion has been filed against 466th District Judge Stephanie Bascon, raising questions about her impartiality in a high-profile case. The motion, submitted by attorneys representing the plaintiff, alleges that Bascon has personal connections to one of the parties involved, potentially compromising her ability to fairly preside over the proceedings.

The case in question involves a contentious land use dispute that has drawn significant local attention. Critics of the motion argue that it is a strategic move to delay the trial, while supporters claim it is necessary to uphold judicial integrity. Bascon has not yet ruled on the recusal request, but legal observers expect a decision within the next few weeks.

In parallel, a voter warning campaign has been launched by a local advocacy group, urging residents to consider Bascon's judicial record when casting ballots in the upcoming election. The campaign highlights several past decisions that the group believes demonstrate bias or poor judgment, though Bascon's supporters counter that her rulings have been consistent with legal precedents.

This dual challenge to Bascon's position comes at a critical time, as she seeks re-election in the March primary. The recusal motion and voter campaign could influence public perception and potentially affect the outcome of the election. Judicial accountability remains a key issue for voters in Comal County, with many emphasizing the importance of impartiality in the court system.

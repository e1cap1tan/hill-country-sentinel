Local Property Owners May Face Higher Tax Bills Under Proposed County Budget

February 14, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

Comal County commissioners are reviewing a proposed budget that would increase property tax rates by 3.2% to fund critical infrastructure projects including road improvements and emergency services expansion.

The current tax rate of 30.5 cents per $100 valuation was increased 15.4% in 2026 from the previous no-new-revenue rate. The proposal comes as the county experiences rapid population growth requiring upgraded public services.

Key details of the proposed budget include:

  • Property tax rate increase of 3.2% proposed on top of current 30.5 cents per $100 valuation
  • Current rate represents 15.4% increase from previous year's rate
  • Funds targeted for road improvements and emergency services
  • County population growth driving infrastructure needs
  • Public hearings scheduled before final budget vote
  • Impact on homeowners estimated at $120 annually for average home

The county's rapid growth has strained existing infrastructure, particularly roads and emergency services. Commissioners are balancing the need to maintain current services while accommodating new development and population increases.

Public hearings are scheduled to allow residents to provide input before commissioners make their final budget decisions.

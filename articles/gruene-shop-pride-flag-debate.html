Gruene Area Shop Displays Pride Flag, Sparks Local Debate

February 10, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

A retail establishment in the Gruene Historic District has drawn community attention after displaying a pride flag, sparking discussion among local residents about businesses taking social positions in the conservative community.

The Gruene Marketplace, located at 1612 Hunter Road in the historic shopping district, began displaying the rainbow flag outside its storefront last week, according to community reports and social media posts.

"I just think businesses should focus on serving customers, not making political statements," said one commenter on a local Facebook community group discussing the display.

The response has been mixed, with some residents expressing support for the business's right to display the flag, while others questioned whether such displays are appropriate in the historic district known for its traditional Texas charm.

Community Discussion

The conversation has taken place primarily on social media platforms, with residents debating the role of businesses in social issues. Some commenters emphasized the importance of inclusivity and business freedom, while others expressed concern about the community's changing character.

"Every customer deserves to feel welcome. Good for them for showing they're an inclusive business," another resident commented on the same Facebook thread.

The Gruene Historic District, established in the 1870s and known for its antique shops, restaurants, and dance hall, has historically maintained a focus on its heritage tourism appeal.

Business Response

The Gruene Marketplace, which specializes in local crafts and Texas-themed merchandise, has not issued a public statement regarding the flag display or the community discussion it has generated.

Similar discussions about business displays have occurred in other Texas communities as businesses navigate expressing values while serving diverse customer bases.

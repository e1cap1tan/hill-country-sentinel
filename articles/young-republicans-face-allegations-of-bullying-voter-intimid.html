Young Republicans Face Allegations of Bullying, Voter Intimidation Ahead of March Primary

February 22, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

NEW BRAUNFELS, TX — The New Braunfels Young Republicans chapter is facing serious allegations of bullying and voter intimidation as the March primary election approaches. Local political activist Joeylynn Mesaros has accused chapter leaders of creating a hostile environment for conservative voters who support certain candidates, particularly in the County Judge race.

Mesaros, who has been vocal about local political issues, claims that chapter president Kristen Hoyt and other members have been pressuring members to support specific candidates and punishing those who express independent views. "This is not conservatism; this is coercion," Mesaros stated in a public post on social media. "The Young Republicans should be about ideas, not intimidation."

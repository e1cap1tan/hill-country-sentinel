Commissioners Court to Discuss Interim County Judge Appointment Feb 26

February 13, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

Comal County Commissioners Court is expected to discuss appointing an interim county judge during their February 26 meeting, following the death of Judge Sherman Krause on February 7, 2026.

The appointment will be crucial for maintaining county operations, as the county judge serves as the chief administrative officer and presiding officer of the commissioners court.

While three Republican candidates are already on the March primary ballot for the full term - former Tax Assessor-Collector Kristen Hoyt, business owner Deb Hindman, and firefighter Kayne Parrish - the interim appointment would serve until the winner of that election takes office.

The commissioners court has the authority to appoint someone to fill the vacancy temporarily, ensuring continuity in county leadership during this transition period.

Judge Krause, who had served Comal County for years, chose not to file for re-election before his passing, making this vacancy particularly significant for the county's governance structure.

Comal ISD Board Examines Enhanced Parental Notification Requirements

February 3, 2026 By Hill Country Sentinel Staff

The Comal Independent School District Board of Trustees conducted a comprehensive review of its parental notification policies during a recent board meeting, focusing on expanding transparency around curriculum changes and library material selections.

The policy review comes as the district works to strengthen communication with families about educational content and materials their children encounter in school. Board members discussed updates that would increase parental awareness of new curriculum adoptions, library acquisitions, and instructional material changes before implementation.

"Parents are their children's first teachers, and they have a right to know what their students are learning," said one board member during the discussion. "These policy updates ensure families can stay informed and engaged in their children's education."

Enhanced Transparency Measures

The proposed policy changes build on existing procedures that already require community input on library materials and curriculum decisions. Under the enhanced notification framework, parents would receive advance notice of new instructional materials, significant curriculum modifications, and additions to school library collections.

Comal ISD currently operates under policies established following the passage of Senate Bill 13, which gave parents and school boards increased oversight over school library books. The district's library materials acquisition policy emphasizes parental voice and board approval for new titles, with librarians consulting professional reviews, age-appropriate ratings, and community feedback.

The board expects to finalize the updated parental notification policies at an upcoming meeting, with implementation planned for the beginning of the next school year. The changes aim to balance transparency with efficient educational operations as the district serves more than 30,300 students across prekindergarten through 12th grade.

Texas cities, school districts, and counties can place bond measures or charter amendments on uniform election dates (May or November). March 3, 2026 ¬∑ Non-Binding Advisory Propositions

Texas Republican Primary Advisory Propositions

These 10 non-binding propositions appear on the March 3, 2026 Republican primary ballot alongside candidate races. They are not laws ‚Äî they are an official opinion poll of Republican voters. The results signal GOP priorities to the Texas Legislature and party leadership, and are expected to shape the agenda at the 2026 Texas GOP Convention in June and the 2027 legislative session. The propositions were approved by the State Republican Executive Committee on Sept. 6, 2025, by a 59‚Äì3 vote.

PROP 1 Property Tax Abolition

"Texas property taxes should be assessed at the purchase price and phased out entirely over the next six years through spending reductions."

A YES vote means: You support eventually eliminating the property tax entirely ‚Äî starting with purchase-price assessment (a "homestead basis" model) and phasing out the tax over six years by cutting government spending rather than raising other taxes.
PROP 2 Voter Approval on Tax Hikes

"Texas should require any local government budget that raises property taxes to be approved by voters at a November general election."

A YES vote means: You want cities, counties, and school districts to get explicit voter approval before raising property taxes ‚Äî putting taxpayers in the driver's seat rather than elected boards who currently raise rates with minimal accountability.
PROP 3 Medical Freedom

"Texas should prohibit denial of healthcare or any medical service based solely on the patient's vaccination status."

A YES vote means: You support protecting Texans from being denied medical treatment simply because they chose not to receive a vaccine ‚Äî a personal liberty issue that gained prominence during COVID-era healthcare mandates.
PROP 4 Pro-Life Education

"Texas should require its public schools to teach that life begins at fertilization."

A YES vote means: You want Texas public school curriculum to reflect the pro-life principle that human life begins at the moment of fertilization ‚Äî aligning public education with Texas's post-Dobbs legal framework and conservative values on human life.
PROP 5 Parental Rights in Schools

"Texas should ban gender, sexuality, and reproductive clinics and services in K-12 schools."

A YES vote means: You support removing gender clinics, sexuality counseling, and reproductive services from K-12 campuses ‚Äî keeping those decisions in the hands of parents and family physicians rather than school systems.
PROP 6 Term Limits

"Texas should enact term limits on all elected officials."

A YES vote means: You want limits on how long any elected official ‚Äî from city council members to state legislators to constitutional officers ‚Äî can serve, ending the career-politician culture and returning government to citizen representation.
PROP 7 Texas Water Sovereignty

"Texas should ban the large-scale export, or sale, of our groundwater and surface water to any single private or public entity."

A YES vote means: You support protecting Texas's water supply from large-scale export deals ‚Äî keeping groundwater and surface water as a resource for Texas communities rather than allowing foreign or out-of-state entities to purchase Texas water in bulk. Critical for fast-growing regions like Comal County.
PROP 8 Border & Immigration

"The Texas Legislature should reduce the burden of illegal immigration on taxpayers by ending public services for illegal immigrants."

A YES vote means: You want state-funded public services limited to legal residents and citizens ‚Äî directing taxpayer dollars toward Texans and those here lawfully, and signaling to the Legislature to pursue policies that reduce the fiscal cost of illegal immigration.
PROP 9 GOP Legislative Majority

"The Republican-controlled Texas Legislature should stop awarding leadership positions, including committee and subcommittee chairmanships and vice chairmanships, to Democrats."

A YES vote means: You believe Republican primary voters ‚Äî who gave the GOP a supermajority in Austin ‚Äî expect Republicans to fully control committee leadership. Democrats currently chair or co-chair committees in the Texas House, a practice many conservatives argue dilutes the mandate voters delivered.
PROP 10 American Law in Texas Courts

"Texas should prohibit Sharia Law."

A YES vote means: You support a statutory or constitutional prohibition ensuring only U.S. and Texas law governs Texas courts and public institutions ‚Äî affirming that American constitutional principles are the supreme legal standard in Texas.
